Compressing a PDF to under 3 MB is the right target for VFS Global visa applications, IRDA-regulated insurance portals, medical document submission systems, and government tender portals that allow larger files than the typical 1–2 MB government exam limit.

The 3 MB range accommodates substantial documents — multi-page bank statements, property ownership records, medical reports, and comprehensive financial proofs — while fitting within the limits of portals designed for larger file handling.

VFS Global handles visa applications for over 65 countries from India, including the UK, Germany, France, Canada, and Australia. Their portal caps most individual supporting documents at 2–3 MB with a total package limit of 3–5 MB per application. Documents that frequently exceed 3 MB and need compression include:

- **6-month bank statements** (scanned) — often 5–15 MB
- **Salary slips** (3–6 months, scanned) — 2–8 MB for scanned copies
- **Property ownership or rent agreement** — 5–20 MB for multi-page agreements
- **Medical reports** for long-term visa applications — 5–20 MB
- **Company registration documents** for business visa applicants

For IRDA insurance portals, policy documents and health declarations that exceed 3 MB are a common pain point during digital claim processing. Many insurer portals built on older infrastructure enforce this exact 3 MB limit.

After compressing to 3 MB, verify that table data (transaction rows in bank statements, medical parameters in health reports) remains legible. For documents with financial figures, zoom in to confirm currency amounts and account numbers are readable before uploading.
