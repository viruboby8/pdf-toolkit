Railway Recruitment Board (RRB) applications for NTPC, Group D, and ALP positions have some of the strictest per-document size limits in Indian government recruitment. Across portals like rrbcdg.gov.in, rrbchennai.gov.in, rrbmumbai.gov.in, and all regional RRB sites, documents are typically capped at 100 KB each, with photos limited to 20–40 KB and signatures to 10–20 KB.

The 100 KB limit is notoriously difficult to meet when candidates use flatbed scanners or phone cameras. A colour scan of a standard A4 Railway pass at 300 DPI produces a file of 1.5–3 MB. Even a black-and-white scan at 150 DPI of a single ITI certificate comes in at 400–600 KB. This is three to six times the portal's limit, and the RRB portals give a file-too-large error with no guidance on how to fix it.

Common Railway documents that need compression before upload:

- **10th mark sheet** — often scanned with colour at high DPI, 500 KB to 2 MB
- **ITI/trade certificate** — frequently scanned by institutions at 600 DPI
- **Railway pass** (for railway employee children) — colour card scan, easily 1–3 MB
- **Caste/community certificate** — issued on letterhead, typically 300 KB–1 MB when scanned
- **Medical fitness certificate** — sometimes multi-page, 500 KB to 2 MB

Scan in greyscale at 150 DPI whenever you can before creating the PDF. If you already have a large PDF, compress it targeting 100 KB. For documents with small print, test the compressed file by zooming in on the certificate number and issuing authority name to ensure they remain readable for the verification officer.
