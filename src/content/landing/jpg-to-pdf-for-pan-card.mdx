Converting JPG photos to PDF for a PAN card application on NSDL's Protean portal or UTI's myutiitsl.com is a required step for anyone who has their identity and address proof documents only as phone photos or image files. Both portals require address proof and identity proof in PDF format under 300 KB.

The most common situation: you have photographed your Aadhaar card, voter ID, or passport bio page on your phone. The photos are in JPG format. You cannot upload JPGs directly to the NSDL portal — it expects a PDF.

The process is:

1. Take clear photos of your address proof document (front and back if relevant)
2. Convert the JPGs to a single PDF using this converter
3. Compress the PDF to under 250 KB (leaving margin below NSDL's 300 KB limit)
4. Upload to the address proof field on the NSDL Protean portal

For Aadhaar card specifically, most people now download the e-Aadhaar PDF from myaadhaar.uidai.gov.in, which is already in PDF format at 100–200 KB. But if you have only a physical Aadhaar card (no UIDAI login), photographing it and converting is the practical alternative.

For voter ID as address proof, photograph both the front (showing your name, father's name, and date of birth) and the back (showing your current address with pincode). These two photos combined into a single 2-page PDF, compressed to under 250 KB, is the correct format for the NSDL portal.

Ensure the photo is taken in daylight or bright indoor light without glare, and all four corners of the document are visible in the frame before converting.
