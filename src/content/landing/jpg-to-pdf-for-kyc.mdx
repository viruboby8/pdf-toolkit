KYC document uploads for stock brokers and investment platforms require your Aadhaar card in PDF format, but a common scenario is having only phone photos (JPG) of your physical Aadhaar card. The e-Aadhaar PDF from UIDAI is password-protected, which many platforms cannot open — making a photo-converted PDF the practical alternative.

The standard KYC Aadhaar document needs two components:

- **Front side**: Aadhaar number, name, date of birth, and photograph
- **Back side**: Current address with pincode

Photograph both sides clearly and convert them here into a single 2-page PDF. This gives the KYC reviewer a complete document in one file. Then compress it to under 500 KB.

The platforms that most commonly require this workflow:

- **Zerodha** — Demat and trading account opening, 1 MB per file limit
- **Groww** — Mutual fund and stock account KYC, 5 MB limit but smaller is better
- **Angel One** — Demat account KYC, 500 KB typical limit
- **Paytm Money** — Mutual fund account KYC
- **Kuvera** and **MFU** — Mutual fund investments requiring CKYC
- **Bank account opening** (Kotak 811, IDFC First, Fi Money) — fully digital KYC

For small finance banks and cooperative banks that require physical KYC documentation, the JPG-to-PDF conversion is also useful when preparing printed sets — the PDF ensures consistent printing across all pages without formatting issues.

One important note: do not upload the password-protected e-Aadhaar PDF from myaadhaar.uidai.gov.in directly. Most KYC systems cannot open it. Use this converter to create an unprotected PDF from physical Aadhaar photos instead.
