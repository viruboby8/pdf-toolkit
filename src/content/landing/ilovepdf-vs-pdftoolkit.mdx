iLovePDF vs PDF Toolkit is a comparison between a comprehensive multi-tool platform and a focused, privacy-first browser application. They overlap on the most common operations but differ significantly on philosophy, privacy, and breadth.

**What iLovePDF does**

iLovePDF offers over twenty PDF operations. Compress, merge, split, rotate are the basics. Beyond those: PDF-to-Word, PDF-to-Excel, PDF-to-PowerPoint, Word/Excel/PowerPoint-to-PDF, PDF repair, PDF redaction, watermarking, page numbering, PDF signing, OCR (paid), and a developer API. It is one of the most comprehensive free PDF platforms available.

**What PDF Toolkit does**

PDF Toolkit covers eleven operations: compress, merge, split, rotate, reorder, delete pages, extract pages, JPG-to-PDF, PDF-to-JPG, OCR (free, client-side), and password removal. This is deliberately focused — the operations that cover roughly 90% of everyday document needs.

**The privacy difference**

iLovePDF processes files on servers in the EU. Files are uploaded, processed, temporarily stored, and then deleted. Their privacy policy is published and GDPR-compliant. For most documents, this is adequate.

PDF Toolkit never uploads files. The merge, compress, OCR, and all other operations run inside WebAssembly in your browser. For documents containing personal identification — Aadhaar, PAN, bank statements, salary slips — no-upload processing is meaningfully more private.

**When to use iLovePDF:** format conversion (PDF-to-Word is iLovePDF's strongest feature), developer API integration, PDF repair, or watermark addition.

**When to use PDF Toolkit:** unlimited, no-account operations for everyday document tasks, privacy-sensitive documents, exact-size compression for Indian government portals, or offline use via PWA.
