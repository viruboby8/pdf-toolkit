Converting a JPG image to a PDF that stays under 100 KB is the standard requirement for several government exam portal uploads in India, particularly for Railway RRB applications and SSC document verification stages. The 100 KB target is one of the tightest size limits you will encounter, and achieving it while keeping the document readable requires both conversion and compression in the same step.

The most common use cases for a JPG-to-PDF conversion targeting 100 KB:

- **Railway RRB NTPC, Group D, ALP** — certificate uploads capped at 100 KB per document
- **SSC CGL, CHSL document verification** — supporting documents often capped at 100–200 KB
- **State PSC applications** — many state commissions (BPSC, MPPSC, RPSC) cap documents at 100–200 KB
- **Bank PO and clerical exam applications** — IBPS and SBI PO portals often require documents under 100–200 KB

The converter handles the full pipeline: it takes your JPG, embeds it in a PDF container, then compresses the image within the PDF to reach the target size. You do not need to separately reduce the JPG quality or resize the image before using this tool.

For best results with the 100 KB target, the original photo should be of a single-page document. Multi-page documents are harder to fit into 100 KB while maintaining legibility — each additional page needs approximately 50–80 KB of its own budget.

After the conversion, verify two things before uploading: that all text in the document is readable when zoomed to 100% on screen, and that the PDF file size on disk is actually under 100 KB (check by right-clicking the file and viewing Properties on Windows, or Get Info on Mac).
