faq:
  - q: What is the file size limit on incometax.gov.in for document uploads?
    a: The income tax portal incometax.gov.in allows individual document uploads up to 50 MB for the main ITR filing, but individual attachment fields for responses to notices, assessment proceedings, and rectification requests are typically capped at 5–10 MB per file. Documents submitted in response to scrutiny notices (Section 143(2), 148, etc.) often have a 2–5 MB per-file limit.
  - q: My Form 16 is 8 MB. Do I need to compress it?
    a: Form 16 from large organisations can be 5–15 MB, especially when it includes Form 16 Part A (TDS details) and Part B (salary breakup) merged into one PDF with company letterhead and signatures. If you are attaching Form 16 to a response to an income tax notice, compress it to under 5 MB. For self-filing purposes (just reading it to fill the ITR), you do not need to compress.
  - q: Can I merge my 26AS and AIS into one PDF and compress it?
    a: Yes. Some tax practitioners merge 26AS, AIS, and TIS into a single document package for record-keeping or to submit as a combined attachment to a CA. You can merge them using the merge PDF tool and then compress the combined file. Individually, 26AS is usually 200–800 KB and AIS is typically 500 KB to 2 MB depending on the number of transactions.
  - q: For submitting documents under scrutiny assessment, what file size is acceptable?
    a: Responses submitted via the e-Proceedings section of the income tax portal typically allow 5 MB per file with a total submission limit of 50 MB per proceeding. However, compress large documents (balance sheets, bank statements, ledgers) to 2–3 MB each to avoid upload timeouts, which are common on the income tax portal during peak season.
  - q: I have multiple TDS certificates (Form 16A) from different deductors. Should I merge and compress?
    a: It depends on how you are filing. For ITR filing, TDS details are entered from Form 16A — you read the values and enter them in the portal. If you need to submit Form 16A copies as supporting documents for an assessment response, merge all Form 16As into one PDF, compress to under 5 MB, and submit as a single attachment with a covering note.

Compressing PDFs for income tax filing and assessment proceedings on incometax.gov.in is most relevant when responding to notices, submitting documents during scrutiny assessments, or maintaining and sharing tax record packages. While the main ITR e-filing process has generous size limits, the e-Proceedings and rectification sections regularly impose tighter per-file constraints.

The most common scenario is a scrutiny assessment under Section 143(3) where the Assessing Officer requests bank statements, investment proofs, and property documents. A 12-month bank statement from SBI or HDFC for a self-employed individual with regular transactions can be 3–8 MB when scanned. Property purchase documents, capital gains computation annexures, and audit reports (where applicable for business income) can each be 5–20 MB.

Tax documents that frequently need compression before portal upload:

- **Form 16** (from employer) — 5–15 MB for large organisations
- **Bank statements** for assessment — 3–10 MB for 12-month statements
- **26AS and AIS** — usually manageable but can be 1–3 MB combined
- **Capital gains statements** from brokers (Zerodha, ICICI Direct, HDFC Securities) — 500 KB to 3 MB
- **Rent receipts or rental income agreements** — particularly if multi-page scans
- **Business balance sheet and P&L** (for audit cases) — often 5–20 MB

For CA-client document exchange, compressing tax document packages to under 5 MB per file makes email sharing practical without using cloud storage links, and keeps the income tax portal submission sessions fast even on moderate internet connections.
