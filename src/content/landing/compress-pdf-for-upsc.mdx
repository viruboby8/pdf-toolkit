UPSC applicants routinely run into file size errors on upsconline.nic.in when uploading the Detailed Application Form (DAF) for CSE Mains or the IFoS application. The portal enforces strict per-document limits: your photograph must be 3–40 KB, your signature 1–30 KB, and most supporting certificates must be under 500 KB to 1 MB each.

The problem is straightforward — modern phone cameras and flatbed scanners produce files far larger than these limits. A single scan of a graduation certificate at 600 DPI in colour can be 5–8 MB. Even a phone photo of a marksheet converted to PDF is often 2–3 MB. UPSC's portal gives no feedback beyond a generic upload error, so many candidates only discover the size issue after repeated failed attempts.

The documents most commonly needing compression for UPSC submissions include:

- **Educational qualification certificates** (graduation, post-graduation) — aim for 500 KB
- **Category/caste certificates** (OBC, SC/ST) — typically 200–500 KB limit
- **Experience certificates** for IFoS — 500 KB to 1 MB
- **Mark sheets** for each year — 200–500 KB each
- **Domicile and income certificates** — usually under 500 KB

For best results, scan documents in greyscale at 150–200 DPI before converting to PDF. If you already have a large scanned PDF, compress it here targeting the specific size the portal demands — 500 KB works for most certificate fields. Always verify the compressed file opens correctly before uploading, as the DAF submission window is time-limited and errors waste precious time during the counselling period.
