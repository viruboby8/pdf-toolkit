Government competitive exam applications — UPSC CSE, SSC CGL, RRB NTPC, and state PSC exams — routinely require supporting documents in PDF format during online registration, document submission, and document verification stages. Candidates who only have phone-photographed JPG images of their certificates need to convert them to PDF before upload.

The requirement is most acute during two phases: initial application registration and document verification (DV). During registration, photo and signature uploads are typically JPEG with strict size caps. But for supporting documents like educational certificates, category certificates, and identity proof, most portals require PDF.

Common documents needing JPG-to-PDF conversion for exam applications:

- **Educational qualification certificates** (graduation, post-graduation) — often photographed rather than scanned
- **Caste/category certificate** (OBC-NCL, SC/ST, EWS) — physical government document
- **Domicile certificate** — state-issued, physical document
- **Experience certificate** — required for UPSC IFoS and many state services exams
- **NOC from employer** — for government employee applicants
- **Character certificate** — issued by a gazetted officer

For UPSC's upsconline.nic.in portal, each document must be a PDF under 500 KB to 1 MB. For SSC portals, supporting documents must be under 200–500 KB. For RRB portals, the cap is 100 KB per document.

After converting your JPG to PDF, compress immediately before uploading. The two-step process — convert, then compress — is the reliable approach. Always test the compressed PDF on your device before the exam portal session to avoid wasted time during limited-window application periods.
