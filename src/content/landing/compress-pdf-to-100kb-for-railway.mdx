Compress PDF to 100 KB for Railway RRB Exams is one of the tightest upload requirements in Indian government recruitment, and it causes a significant number of application failures among candidates who do not account for it until the document upload screen.

Railway Recruitment Board portals — rrbcdg.gov.in, rrbmumbai.gov.in, rrbsecunderabad.gov.in, and the other 19 RRB zone portals — enforce a 100 KB limit on category certificates and ID proof PDFs during the document upload stage of RRB NTPC 2025 and RRB Group D 2025 applications. This limit is among the strictest imposed by any central government recruitment body.

Caste certificates, OBC non-creamy layer certificates, and disability certificates are typically multi-colour, multi-stamp official documents. When scanned at reasonable quality on a smartphone, they produce PDFs of 1-4 MB. Even scanner apps optimised for documents — CamScanner, Adobe Scan, Microsoft Lens — produce files well above 100 KB.

Generic compression approaches fail here because they do not target a specific output size. A tool that "compresses by 60%" takes a 3 MB file to 1.2 MB — still twelve times the limit. PDF Toolkit's binary-search compression targets exactly 100 KB, iterating until the output qualifies.

The compression does not touch vector text — only the embedded raster image. Typed text remains sharp. Official seals may show slight JPEG artefacts at extreme compression but remain identifiable for verification.

After compressing, open the file in a PDF viewer at 100% zoom. If the issuing authority's seal, the candidate's name, and the certificate number are readable, the file is submission-ready.
