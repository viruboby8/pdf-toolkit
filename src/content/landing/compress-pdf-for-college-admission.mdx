  - q: What is the document size limit on JoSAA for 2026-27?
    a: JoSAA (Joint Seat Allocation Authority) requires each uploaded document to be under 200 KB in PDF format for the 2026-27 admission cycle. This covers your Class 10 mark sheet, Class 12 mark sheet, category certificate, and PwD certificate if applicable. Check the JoSAA official notice for the exact cycle you are applying in, as limits can be revised.
  - q: The DU admission portal is rejecting my marksheet PDF. What should I do?
    a: Delhi University's admission portal typically requires PDFs under 200 KB to 500 KB depending on the document type. Phone-photographed marksheets converted to PDF are usually 1–3 MB. Compress the PDF to 200 KB here, then try uploading again. If the portal still rejects it, ensure the file extension is .pdf (not .PDF or .jpeg).
  - q: I have a multi-page marksheet (4 pages for 4 semesters). How do I compress it?
    a: A 4-page colour scanned marksheet can be 2–5 MB. Compress it targeting 300–400 KB. The compressor will reduce the image resolution of each page proportionally. For 4 pages at 300–400 KB total, each page ends up around 75–100 KB which is still legible for mark-by-mark verification.
  - q: State counselling portals (JOSAA, MHT-CET, KCET) — do they have the same limits?
    a: No, each state counselling authority sets its own limits. MHT-CET ARO typically allows 500 KB per document. KCET (Karnataka) usually caps documents at 200 KB. TNEA (Tamil Nadu) allows up to 500 KB. Always read the specific counselling notice for your state and adjust the target size accordingly.
  - q: Should I compress my JEE or NEET scorecard PDF?

College admission portals in India for the 2026-27 academic year enforce tight document size limits that catch many students during the document upload phase of JoSAA, DU CSAS, and state-level counselling processes. JoSAA specifically requires PDFs under 200 KB per document, while DU's portal and most state counselling platforms cap files at 200–500 KB depending on the document category.

The challenge is that marksheets and certificates provided by CBSE, state boards, and universities are often issued as large scanned PDFs or, in many cases, students photograph them with their phone camera and convert them to PDF. A phone photo of a Class 12 CBSE marksheet taken under normal indoor lighting and converted to PDF is typically 2–5 MB — well above the 200 KB JoSAA limit.

Documents typically required by admission portals that need compression:

- **Class 10 mark sheet** — CBSE, ICSE, or state board, often 1–3 MB as scanned
- **Class 12 mark sheet** — may be multi-page, 2–5 MB from phone photos
- **Provisional/passing certificate** — single page but high DPI scan can be 500 KB–2 MB
- **Category certificate** (OBC-NCL, SC/ST, EWS) — 300 KB–1 MB
- **Domicile certificate** — 300 KB–1 MB when scanned in colour

Compress each document individually, targeting 150–180 KB for JoSAA uploads. For state counselling, target 400 KB to be safe. After compressing, zoom into the compressed PDF on your screen to confirm all grades, subject names, and roll numbers remain legible — admission verification officers will check these details carefully during the reporting process.
