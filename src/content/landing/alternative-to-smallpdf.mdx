PDF Toolkit vs Smallpdf is a comparison worth making honestly, because both tools have real strengths and the right choice depends on what you are doing.

Smallpdf is a polished, comprehensive product. Its UI is well-designed, it supports over twenty file operations including PDF-to-Word and PDF-to-Excel conversion, and it integrates with Google Drive and Dropbox for cloud-based workflows. It is a reasonable choice for someone who needs those features and is comfortable with server-side processing.

Smallpdf's free tier limits you to two tasks per day and requires an account or Google sign-in. For occasional use with non-sensitive documents, this may be acceptable.

PDF Toolkit takes a different approach. Processing is entirely client-side — your files never leave your device. There is no account, no task limit, no daily cap, and no watermark. It is MIT open-source.

The specific feature PDF Toolkit provides for Indian government portal use: target-size compression. Smallpdf reduces file size by a percentage without guaranteeing a specific KB output. PDF Toolkit's binary-search approach iterates until the output meets your exact target — critical for portals that reject files above 100 KB or 200 KB.

Where Smallpdf wins clearly: PDF-to-Word conversion, e-signing, and cloud storage integration. PDF Toolkit does not do format conversion.

Use PDF Toolkit for privacy-sensitive documents, unlimited compression, exact size targets, and offline use. Use Smallpdf for format conversion and cloud-integrated workflows.
