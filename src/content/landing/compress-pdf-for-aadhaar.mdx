Updating your address or name on Aadhaar through the online portal myaadhaar.uidai.gov.in requires uploading a supporting proof document as a PDF. The portal accepts files up to 2 MB, but large scanned documents — particularly multi-page utility bills and bank passbooks — routinely exceed this limit.

The most common Aadhaar update scenario involves electricity or water bills. Households that receive quarterly or bi-monthly bills often scan 2–4 pages together, producing colour PDFs of 3–8 MB. Gas connection documents from providers like Indane or HP Gas can similarly be large when scanned at the default 600 DPI setting. Even a single-page bank statement photographed on a phone with flash can produce a 3–4 MB PDF after conversion.

Documents most frequently needing compression for Aadhaar updates:

- **Electricity bill** (MSEDCL, BESCOM, TPDDL, etc.) — 3–8 MB when multi-page colour scan
- **Gas connection book** — often photographed rather than scanned, 2–5 MB
- **Bank passbook** — first page + recent transaction page, typically 1–3 MB
- **Water bill or property tax receipt** — colour scan often exceeds 2 MB
- **Registered rent/lease agreement** — multi-page, easily 5–15 MB

For Aadhaar specifically, only the page showing your name, current address, and the issuing authority matters. If your document is multi-page, consider extracting the relevant page first using the PDF split tool, then compress the single page to under 500 KB. This also speeds up the UIDAI verification process since reviewers only need to check the address field against what you submitted in the portal form.
