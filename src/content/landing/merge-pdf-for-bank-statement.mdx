Merge PDF Bank Statements for Loan Applications solves a friction point that nearly every loan applicant encounters: each month's bank statement downloads as a separate PDF from internet banking, but the loan application portal — or the loan officer — wants all months in one file.

Indian banks and NBFCs including HDFC Bank, ICICI Bank, State Bank of India, Axis Bank, and Bajaj Finserv require bank statements covering the last three to six months as part of loan processing. For a home loan or personal loan, this commonly means six separate PDF files — one per month — that need to be submitted together.

Internet banking portals of most banks do not offer a "download multiple months as one PDF" option. SBI's YONO app generates one PDF per statement period. HDFC NetBanking's e-statement download is similarly per-month. You must download each month individually and then combine them.

PDF Toolkit's merge tool handles this: add all six PDFs, arrange them in chronological order, and download the merged file.

One step that catches many applicants: Indian bank e-statements are password-protected. SBI uses the account holder's date of birth in DDMMYYYY format. HDFC and ICICI use a combination of name and date of birth. Use PDF Toolkit's unlock tool to remove each password before merging.

After merging, check the file size. Six months of SBI statements can total 15-30 MB. Loan portals often limit uploads to 5-10 MB — compress the merged PDF before uploading.

Because bank statements contain account numbers, balances, and transaction history, client-side processing means this financial data never leaves your device.
