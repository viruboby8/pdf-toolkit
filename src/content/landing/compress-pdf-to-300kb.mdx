Compressing a PDF to under 300 KB is a specific requirement for PAN card address proof uploads on NSDL's Protean portal, and it appears on a range of other portals including several state-level civil services exam registrations, Sarathi Parivahan for driving licence applications, and select university document portals.

The 300 KB sweet spot gives you meaningfully better image quality than the 100–200 KB range while still meeting portals that have tighter limits than the typical 500 KB government standard. A single-page scanned document at 300 KB has approximately 150 DPI effective resolution — good enough for all verification purposes.

Documents most commonly needing compression to 300 KB:

- **Address proof for PAN card** (NSDL Protean) — rental agreements, utility bills, Aadhaar scans
- **State civil services exam certificates** — RPSC (Rajasthan), MPPSC (Madhya Pradesh), BPSC (Bihar) portals
- **Sarathi Parivahan driving licence** — address and identity proof depending on state configuration
- **University admission portals** — several private and state universities cap documents at 300 KB
- **Bank account KYC** — some cooperative bank portals use 300 KB as their upload cap

For the NSDL PAN card use case specifically: if your address proof is a digital PDF (bank statement downloaded from net banking, e-Aadhaar from UIDAI), it is likely already under 300 KB. Compression is mainly needed when the document is a scanned physical copy or a phone photo converted to PDF. Scan or photograph, compress to 250–280 KB, and verify all address text is readable before submitting.
