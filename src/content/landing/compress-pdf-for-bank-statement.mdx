Bank statement PDFs are among the most commonly uploaded documents in home loan, personal loan, and NBFC loan applications — and also among the most frequently rejected for being too large. Lenders' online portals, from SBI YONO to HDFC Bank's iLend platform to digital NBFCs like Bajaj Finserv and Tata Capital, consistently cap bank statement uploads at 1–2 MB.

The size problem stems from how applicants obtain their statements. Branches and passbook counters still issue physical copies, which applicants then scan. A 6-month SBI or PNB passbook covering 60–80 transaction pages, scanned at 300 DPI in colour, easily runs 15–30 MB. Even a 3-month statement from a busy current account can be 8–12 MB.

Digital bank statements — downloaded as PDF from net banking — are a much better starting point. HDFC SmartStatement, SBI net banking e-statements, and ICICI's digital statement downloads are usually 500 KB to 2 MB for 6 months because they are computer-generated PDFs, not image scans. If your digital statement is still over 2 MB (happens with business current accounts having hundreds of transactions), compressing it to 1 MB works without any loss.

For scanned passbook statements, compression to 1 MB for 6 months is achievable. For 12-month statements with 100+ pages, target 2 MB. Before submitting:

- Verify all salary credit entries or business inflows are clearly readable
- Confirm the account holder name and account number are visible on every page
- Check the bank stamp or digital signature footer is visible (lenders look for this)

Some digital lenders now pull statements directly via Account Aggregator (AA) framework, which bypasses the upload size issue entirely. Check if the lender offers this option before compressing.
