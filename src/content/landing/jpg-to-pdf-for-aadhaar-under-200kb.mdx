Convert JPG to PDF Under 200 KB for Aadhaar covers a two-step workflow that many users encounter when updating their address or other details on myaadhaar.uidai.gov.in: they have photographs of their supporting documents as JPG files, the portal requires a PDF, and the resulting file must meet a size threshold.

The myaadhaar.uidai.gov.in self-service portal allows residents to update their name, date of birth, gender, address, and mobile number without visiting an Aadhaar Seva Kendra. Address updates require uploading supporting documentation — a bank statement, utility bill, voter ID, or other UIDAI-approved address proof.

When the supporting document is a physical paper (a landlord's declaration, a manually issued utility bill), the practical way to digitise it is to photograph it with a smartphone. But the portal requires PDF format. Converting the JPG to PDF is the bridge step.

PDF Toolkit's JPG-to-PDF tool accepts one or more image files and packages them into a multi-page PDF. If you are uploading an Aadhaar card as address proof for another application, add both the front and back side images to produce a two-page PDF.

After conversion, the PDF typically needs to be compressed. A JPG photograph from a modern smartphone is 2-5 MB; the PDF wrapping it is roughly the same. The UIDAI portal's general limit is 2 MB, and some upload slots cap at 200 KB.

The workflow: photograph your document → convert JPG to PDF → if the PDF exceeds the limit, compress to your required size → upload to myaadhaar.uidai.gov.in.
