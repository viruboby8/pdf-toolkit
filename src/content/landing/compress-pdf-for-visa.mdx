Visa application portals — whether for Indian eVisa, Schengen applications via VFS Global, UK Visas and Immigration, or individual embassy upload systems — consistently set per-document size limits that can block submissions of bank statements, hotel bookings, travel insurance, and financial documents.

VFS Global, which handles visa applications for over 65 countries including the UK, Germany, France, Australia, and Canada, caps individual file uploads at 2–3 MB with a typical total submission package limit of 3–5 MB. The Indian eVisa portal at indianvisaonline.gov.in accepts most supporting documents at up to 1 MB each.

The problem arises mainly with financial documents. A 6-month bank statement from SBI, HDFC, or ICICI downloaded as a digital PDF is typically 300–800 KB — within limits. But a scanned paper statement runs 3–10 MB. Similarly, property documents submitted for financial backing proof can be 5–20 MB.

Documents most frequently needing compression for visa applications:

- **Bank statements** (3–6 months) — scanned copies can be 3–10 MB
- **Property ownership documents or fixed deposit certificates** — often large scans
- **Salary slips or Form 16** — particularly if scanned from paper
- **Hotel booking confirmations** (if printed and re-scanned) — 500 KB to 2 MB
- **Travel insurance certificates** — usually fine if digital, 500 KB–2 MB if scanned

Compress each document to under 500 KB before starting your VFS or embassy upload session. This gives you headroom even if the portal's stated limit is 2 MB, and avoids session timeouts caused by slow file uploads during the online application.
