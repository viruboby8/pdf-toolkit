faq:
  - q: What is the file size limit for PAN card document uploads on NSDL?
    a: The NSDL Protean portal (onlineservices.nsdl.com) requires address proof and identity proof PDFs to be under 300 KB each. The photograph must be under 50 KB and the signature under 30 KB. UTI's myutiitsl.com portal has similar limits. Always check the specific requirement shown on the upload page as these can be updated.
  - q: My Aadhaar PDF downloaded from UIDAI is already a few hundred KB — do I need to compress it?
    a: The e-Aadhaar PDF downloaded from myaadhaar.uidai.gov.in is usually 100–200 KB and should fall within the NSDL limit. However, if you have a scanned physical Aadhaar card or a photo of it converted to PDF, that file is often 500 KB to 2 MB and will need compression to under 300 KB.
  - q: My rental agreement is a 30-page scanned PDF and it's 15 MB. Can I compress it for PAN?
    a: Yes, but consider also extracting only the first few pages — typically the cover page and the page showing your current address. A 30-page agreement at 500 KB is unnecessary. Extract the 2–3 relevant pages, then compress. The NSDL portal only needs sufficient proof of your address, not the entire document.
  - q: Will NSDL reject a compressed PDF?
    a: No. NSDL and UTI portals accept any valid PDF under the stated size limit. Compression does not alter the document structure — the portal cannot detect or reject a file simply because it has been compressed.
  - q: Which documents are accepted as address proof for PAN card applications?
    a: Accepted address proofs include Aadhaar card, passport, voter ID, driving licence, bank account statement (issued within 3 months), utility bills (electricity, water, gas — within 3 months), and registered rent agreement. For all of these, the PDF must be under 300 KB for NSDL upload.

Applying for a new PAN card or making corrections to an existing PAN through the NSDL Protean portal or UTI's myutiitsl.com requires uploading your identity proof and address proof as PDF files under 300 KB. This catches many applicants off guard when their scanned documents or converted images exceed the limit.

The most frequent cause of oversized files is rental agreement scans. Landlords or property consultants often provide scanned agreements as multi-page PDFs at 300 DPI in colour — a 20-page agreement can easily be 10–15 MB. For PAN purposes, you only need 2–3 pages: the first page showing both parties' names, and the page showing the address with your name. Extract those pages first, then compress the extracted PDF to under 300 KB.

The other common issue is Aadhaar PDF proofs. The official e-Aadhaar from UIDAI is usually fine at 100–200 KB. But many people scan their physical Aadhaar card or take a phone photo and convert it to PDF, resulting in 500 KB to 2 MB files.

Documents commonly needed for PAN that require compression:

- **Aadhaar card PDF** (scanned physical card) — compress to under 200 KB
- **Rental agreement** — extract relevant pages then compress to under 300 KB
- **Bank statement** (for address proof) — recent 1-month statement, target 200–300 KB
- **Electricity or gas bill** — compress to under 200 KB
- **Passport bio page** — compress to under 200 KB

After compressing, open the PDF and zoom in to verify your full address and name are clearly readable before uploading to NSDL or UTI.
