SSC CGL, CHSL, MTS, and Stenographer registrations on ssc.nic.in come with some of the tightest file size limits of any central government exam portal in India. The photo cap sits between 20 KB and 50 KB, the signature between 10 KB and 20 KB, and supporting documents must usually be under 100 KB to 500 KB each depending on the document type and the specific SSC examination notification.

The challenge is that these limits apply across several different portals. SSC's central registration portal (ssc.nic.in) handles the main application, but regional offices — SSC CR (Central Region), SSC NR (Northern Region), SSC ER (Eastern Region), SSC SR (Southern Region), and others — each host their own document verification and DV stage portals, sometimes with slightly different rules. Candidates often find their documents rejected at DV stage because they did not notice a regional portal's tighter cap.

The most commonly over-sized documents at SSC stages include:

- **10th and 12th mark sheets** — multi-page, often scanned in colour at high DPI
- **Graduation certificates** and **provisional degree certificates**
- **OBC/SC/ST/EWS category certificates** — government-issued, sometimes poorly scanned
- **Experience/NOC letters** for departmental candidates
- **Disability certificates** for PwBD applicants

To stay safe across all SSC portals, aim for 200 KB or under for every document. A compressed PDF at 200 KB is well within any SSC limit and keeps image quality acceptable for printed verification copies during the document verification round.
