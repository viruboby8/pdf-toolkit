Merge PDF for UPSC Application is a practical necessity for candidates preparing the Detailed Application Form (DAF) for UPSC Civil Services Examination (CSE) and Indian Forest Service (IFoS). The DAF is submitted after clearing the Preliminary examination, and it requires uploading scanned copies of all academic and personal documents that support the information filled in the form.

A typical UPSC CSE DAF document set includes: graduation marksheets for each year (three or four separate PDFs), the provisional degree or final result certificate, caste certificate, domicile certificate, gap year certificate if applicable, and for candidates claiming educational qualification under exceptional categories, additional supporting documents. This easily totals eight to fifteen separate files.

UPSC's DAF portal (upsconline.gov.in) has specific upload slots for different document categories. Within certain slots, combining related documents into one file is required or at least simpler than multiple uploads to a single field. Merging all annual marksheets into one file before uploading to the educational qualification slot is the standard approach.

Using PDF Toolkit for this: add all relevant PDFs in the correct order (first year, second year, third year, final result), drag to confirm the sequence, and download the merged file. The output is a standard PDF that UPSC's portal accepts without issue.

After merging, check the file size. UPSC typically limits uploads to 1-2 MB per slot. A merged set of four marksheet PDFs can easily be 5-10 MB if scanned at high resolution. Use the compress tool after merging, targeting the portal's stated limit.

DigiLocker-issued PDFs — which include digitally signed marksheets from many state boards and universities, and central certificates like birth certificates — are fully compatible and can be merged alongside scanned documents.
