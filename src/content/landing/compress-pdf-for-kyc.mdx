KYC document uploads for stockbrokers, mutual fund platforms, and bank account openings are gated behind file size limits that catch many users during onboarding. The Central KYC Registry (CKYC) caps uploads at 500 KB per document, and platforms like Zerodha, Groww, Angel One, and Paytm Money enforce similar limits within their own systems.

The typical KYC document set includes Aadhaar card (front and back), PAN card, and sometimes a separate address proof or bank statement. The challenge varies by how you obtain these:

- **E-Aadhaar PDF from UIDAI** — usually 100–200 KB, no compression needed
- **Photographed Aadhaar card** converted to PDF — typically 500 KB to 2 MB
- **Scanned PAN card** — 300 KB to 1 MB
- **Bank statement** (for address proof) — 500 KB to 5 MB depending on statement length
- **Signature image** converted to PDF — usually fine at under 100 KB

The signature and photo requirements for Demat account opening add another dimension. Angel One and IIFL require a clear signature on white paper, photographed and uploaded as PDF or JPEG. A high-resolution phone photo at full quality can be 3–5 MB even for a simple signature scan. Compress it to under 200 KB.

For mutual fund investments through platforms like MFU or Kuvera, the CKYC-linked process requires a single submission. Compress each document to under 400 KB before starting the KYC form to avoid having to restart the onboarding session if an upload fails partway through.
