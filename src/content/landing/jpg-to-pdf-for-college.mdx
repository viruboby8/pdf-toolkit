Converting JPG photos of marksheets, certificates, and identity documents to PDF is an essential step for college admission document uploads on JoSAA, DU CSAS, and state counselling portals. These platforms uniformly require PDF format and reject JPEG or PNG uploads, but most students in India photograph their documents with their phone camera, resulting in JPG files.

The scenario is particularly common for students from schools and colleges that issue paper mark sheets without digital alternatives. CBSE and ICSE provide DigiLocker access to digital mark sheets, but state board students (BSEB, UP Board, Maharashtra Board, Tamil Nadu Board) often rely on physical mark sheets that they photograph.

Documents that need JPG-to-PDF conversion for college admissions:

- **Class 10 mark sheet** — state board physical copy photographed
- **Class 12 mark sheet** — especially for boards without DigiLocker integration
- **Transfer certificate (TC)** — issued on physical paper by school
- **Migration certificate** — required by many universities for students from other states
- **Caste/category certificate** — government-issued, usually a physical document
- **Domicile certificate** — state-issued, rarely available digitally

For JoSAA's 200 KB limit, take the photo at your phone's medium quality setting to get a smaller starting file. Good lighting (natural daylight, no flash) and a flat surface ensure the board seal and all printed text appear sharp even at compressed file sizes. After converting and compressing, always do a final check — zoom in on the document number and marks — before submitting during the JoSAA choice-filling window.
