PDF Toolkit vs iLovePDF is a comparison between two tools with fundamentally different philosophies: comprehensive feature breadth versus focused client-side privacy.

**Features**

iLovePDF offers over twenty distinct PDF operations. Beyond the standard compress, merge, and split, it includes PDF-to-Word, PDF-to-Excel, PDF-to-PowerPoint, watermarking, page numbering, PDF repair, and a developer API. It is one of the most complete free-tier PDF suites available.

PDF Toolkit covers eleven operations: compress, merge, split, rotate, reorder, delete pages, extract pages, JPG-to-PDF, PDF-to-JPG, OCR, and password removal. This covers the vast majority of everyday document tasks without format conversion.

**Privacy**

iLovePDF processes files on their servers in Spain and the EU. Files are transmitted over HTTPS and deleted after processing according to their published privacy policy. For general documents this is acceptable. For documents containing personal identification numbers, financial data, or medical information, server-side processing introduces unnecessary exposure.

PDF Toolkit processes everything in the browser. Files never reach any server. For Aadhaar cards, PAN cards, salary slips, and bank statements — the documents most commonly compressed before portal uploads in India — this is a meaningful privacy advantage.

**Cost and limits**

iLovePDF's free tier includes ads and some feature restrictions. PDF Toolkit has no ads, no tiers, and no usage limits.

**When to use iLovePDF:** you need PDF-to-Word conversion, PDF repair, page watermarking, or a developer API. Those are genuine capabilities PDF Toolkit does not have.

**When to use PDF Toolkit:** you need unlimited compression, merge, or split operations without uploading files, on any device, with no account required.
