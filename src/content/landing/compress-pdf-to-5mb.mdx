Compressing a PDF to under 5 MB is the relevant target for several professional and legal document submission contexts in India. Court e-filing systems, government tender portals, and income tax assessment response submissions all commonly enforce a 5 MB per-file cap — significantly more generous than government exam portals, but still restrictive for high-resolution scanned documents.

The documents that most frequently need compression to 5 MB include:

**Court filings** — Indian High Courts using the efiling.ecourts.gov.in system cap each document upload at 5 MB. Writ petitions, affidavit bundles, and typed submissions with annexures can run 20–50 MB when each exhibit is a high-resolution scan.

**Government tenders** — GeM (Government e-Marketplace) and CPPP tender documents like bid proposals, company registration certificates, financial statements, and technical brochures often exceed 5 MB when submitted as high-quality PDFs.

**Income tax assessment** — Responses to Section 143(2) scrutiny notices and 148 reassessment notices require attaching financial statements, bank statements, and supporting computations via the income tax e-Proceedings portal, which allows up to 5 MB per file.

**Architectural and engineering submissions** — Large-format technical drawings submitted to government authorities (RERA, municipal corporations, PWD) are often 20–50 MB per sheet and must be compressed for portal submission.

For court filings specifically, ensure that typed text in judgements cited as annexures, and printed exhibit stamps, remain clearly readable after compression. The court registry reviewer and the judge's staff need to read exhibit markings and dates. Zoom in to verify these are sharp before filing.
