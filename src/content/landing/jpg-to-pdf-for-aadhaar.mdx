Converting JPG photos to PDF for Aadhaar address proof upload on myaadhaar.uidai.gov.in is one of the most common document preparation tasks for Aadhaar updates in India. The portal only accepts PDF format, but most people have phone photos (JPG or PNG) of their address proof documents — utility bills, voter IDs, driving licences, or bank passbooks.

The typical workflow for Aadhaar address update is:

1. Photograph your address proof document — both sides if required (e.g., voter ID front and back)
2. Convert the JPG photos to a single PDF using this tool
3. Compress the PDF to under 2 MB if it exceeds the UIDAI portal limit
4. Upload on myaadhaar.uidai.gov.in under the "Update Demographics" section

The key advantage of combining both sides into a single PDF is that the UIDAI system expects one document per proof type. Uploading two separate files for the front and back of a voter ID typically confuses the portal. A 2-page PDF with front and back correctly ordered is the right format.

Photograph quality matters for UIDAI acceptance. The reviewing officer needs to read:

- **Your full name** as it appears on the address proof
- **Current address** with pincode
- **Issue date or billing period** (for utility bills, must be within 3 months)
- **Issuing authority** name or stamp

Take photos with good lighting, keep the entire document in frame, and avoid shadows across the text. If you are photographing a utility bill, photograph only the section showing your name and address — this reduces file size and makes the relevant content easier for the reviewer to find.
