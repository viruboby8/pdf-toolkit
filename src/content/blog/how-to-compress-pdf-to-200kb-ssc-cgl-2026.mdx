SSC CGL 2026 registration is live on ssc.nic.in, and the portal is strict about file sizes. Every certificate upload — 10th marksheet, 12th marksheet, category certificate, EWS certificate — must be under 200 KB. If you exceed it, the portal shows a red error and refuses the upload. Here is the exact process to get any PDF under 200 KB in under two minutes.

## Why PDFs from scanners and phones are too large

A standard Android phone camera set to auto quality produces JPEGs at 3–5 MB per shot. Even phone-scanner apps like CamScanner compress to PDF at 300–600 KB per page by default. A two-page marksheet scanned this way typically lands at 800 KB–1.2 MB — four to six times over the SSC portal limit.

## Step-by-step compression for SSC CGL 2026

**Step 1 — Open your PDF in PDF Toolkit's compress tool.** Drop the file into the page. No upload, no account needed.

**Step 2 — Select the 200 KB preset.** The tool uses binary search over JPEG quality, running up to six iterations to land at exactly your target. Most scanned documents compress successfully in under 30 seconds on a mid-range phone.

**Step 3 — Check the output file size.** The tool reports the output size before you download. If it reads 198 KB or similar, you are good. Download and upload directly to ssc.nic.in.

## What to do if compression cannot reach 200 KB

If your document is a very long multi-page scan at high resolution, a single pass may not reach 200 KB. Solutions:

- **Split the PDF first** — if the portal accepts separate files per certificate, split your scan into individual pages using the split tool and compress each separately.
- **Re-scan at lower resolution** — 150 DPI is sufficient for text legibility; avoid 600 DPI scans for government form uploads.
- **Remove blank pages** — many phone-scanner outputs include a blank reverse side; delete those pages before compressing.

## Don't forget the photo and signature

SSC CGL applications also require a separate photo JPG (10–50 KB) and signature JPG (4–30 KB). These are separate uploads, not included in the certificate PDF. If your photo or signature is being submitted as a PDF for some reason, use this same compress tool to bring it under the limit.

The 200 KB limit applies to each individual document — not a combined total. Compressing each file separately is the correct workflow.
