The myaadhaar.uidai.gov.in address update portal accepts only PDF files for proof of address. If you have a scanned electricity bill, rental agreement, or bank passbook as JPG images, you cannot upload them directly. You need to convert to PDF first — and the portal caps the file at 2 MB.

## Documents accepted as address proof on myaadhaar.uidai.gov.in

The UIDAI accepts 32 documents as address proof. Common ones that people typically have as JPG scans:

- **Electricity / gas / telephone bill** (must be less than 3 months old)
- **Bank account passbook** (front page with address)
- **Rental or lease agreement** (registered)
- **Voter ID card** (front + back)
- **Driving licence** (front + back)
- **Passport** (front + back pages)

Multi-page documents (a passbook with 4 pages, a lease agreement with 8 pages) are the most common reason people end up with multiple JPG files to combine.

## Converting JPG to PDF without installing software

PDF Toolkit handles this entirely in your browser:

1. Open the JPG to PDF tool on this site.
2. Drop all your JPG images — front side, back side, all pages.
3. Drag thumbnails to arrange them in the correct order (front before back, page 1 before page 2).
4. Click **Convert to PDF**. The tool creates one PDF with one page per image.
5. Download the PDF.

If the resulting PDF exceeds 2 MB (common with high-resolution phone photos), compress it: open the compress tool, set the target to 2 MB, and download the compressed version.

## Why phone photos are often too large

A modern Android phone camera at default settings produces 3–8 MB JPEG files. A 5-page passbook photographed at full resolution = 25 MB worth of images → 15–20 MB PDF before any compression. The UIDAI portal will reject this.

The fix is not to take lower-quality photos — you need the document to be readable for UIDAI verification. Instead, compress after conversion using the 2 MB preset. The compression algorithm re-encodes only the image data inside the PDF, preserving all text legibility while reducing file size.

## After uploading — processing time

Once you upload the address proof PDF on myaadhaar.uidai.gov.in, UIDAI typically completes the address update within 30 days. You'll receive an SMS on your registered mobile. No UIDAI centre visit is needed for address changes on documents that match the pre-verified list.
